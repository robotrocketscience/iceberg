# Project ICEBERG — Level 0 Requirements

**Status:** draft v0.13, 2026-05-21.
**Scope:** project-level (Level 0) requirements. Implementation- and technology-neutral. Each requirement is a SHALL statement with a verifiable metric and a verification method.
**Audience:** the design flows downstream from this document. When a design cannot meet a requirement, the requirement is either re-negotiated explicitly (with rationale recorded here) or the design is rejected. **The requirement is the contract.**

## Conventions

- **SHALL** = mandatory.
- **SHOULD** = strongly preferred; deviation requires written rationale.
- **MAY** = permitted.
- Verification methods: **T** = Test, **A** = Analysis, **D** = Demonstration, **I** = Inspection.
- Each requirement carries an identifier of the form `L0-NN`. Children at Level 1 will use `L1-NN.M`.
- "Customer" means the entity to which delivered water is handed off. Could be a fuel depot, a station, a private operator. Does not refer to ICEBERG's owner.

## 1. Mission objective

| ID | Requirement | Metric | Verification |
|---|---|---|---|
| L0-01 | Project ICEBERG SHALL harvest water ice from Saturn's main ring system. | At least one mission delivers Saturn-ring-sourced water ice to Earth orbit, verified by mass-spectrometry isotopic signature matching Saturn-ring D/H ratio (1.7 × 10⁻⁴ ± 30%, per Cassini Ion and Neutral Mass Spectrometer 2008 baseline) and distinguishable from terrestrial, lunar, and carbonaceous-chondrite reference signatures. | T |
| L0-02 | Project ICEBERG SHALL deliver harvested water to customers in low Earth orbit or medium Earth orbit. | At least 95% of harvested mass delivered to a customer-specified orbit with altitude between 250 kilometres (low Earth orbit floor) and 35,786 kilometres (geostationary altitude, medium Earth orbit ceiling). Verified per-mission by on-orbit mass-transfer record. (Restricting destination to low and medium Earth orbit places water where the largest near-term customer population sits and where rendezvous-and-handoff infrastructure can be demonstrated incrementally. Cislunar and beyond are out of scope at Level 0; if a cislunar customer later emerges, they are responsible for the transfer from delivery orbit to their location.) | T, A |
| L0-03 | Project ICEBERG SHALL deliver water in a form usable by customers without further processing. | Purity ≥ 99% H₂O by mass at delivery interface, with trace contaminants below limits compatible with downstream electrolysis (no metals > 1 ppm, no organics > 100 ppm). Verified by sampling at customer hand-off. | T |

## 2. Performance

| ID | Requirement | Metric | Verification |
|---|---|---|---|
| L0-04 | Each steady-state commercial delivery mission SHALL place no less than the mission-class floor of water at the customer interface. | Per-mission delivered mass ≥ mission-class floor as defined in section 7.5 (Mission classes). **Initial steady-state commercial-class floor (v0.13): 25 tonnes, bracketed by engineering ceiling (~30 t per assumption audit; see below) and demonstrator-class threshold (10 t per §7.5).** Measured by mass-balance at customer hand-off. The floor for commercial-class missions is the smallest delivered mass for which the program net present value (L0-13) remains positive at the contracted operating cadence; it is an output of the financial model, not an a priori input, and is expected to evolve as launch costs and customer pricing evolve. **The 25-tonne provisional value is the engineering-feasible upper bound — not yet validated against an operative financial model.** Sensitivity analysis per `water-prop/rounds/R_assumption_audit_2026_05_21/FINDINGS.md` finds that the closure surface is a cliff in this region: at canonical architecture with chunk_mass = 200 t, water-electrothermal specific impulse = 800 s, lunar-orbit processing sub-mission active, the engineering surface admits L0-04 ≤ ~30 t (52% of feasible paths close at 10-tonne floor; 25% at 20 t; 8.5% at 30 t; 0% at 50 t; 0% at 100 t). **L0-04 ≥ 50 t is infeasible at the current architecture.** Provisional 25 t selected to leave a 5-tonne margin against the engineering ceiling pending operative financial-model derivation. | T, A |
| L0-05 | Round-trip time per mission SHALL NOT exceed 15 years from Earth departure to Earth-orbit delivery. | Per-mission round-trip ≤ 15 years, measured Earth-departure to delivery hand-off. (Selected from a financial-model analysis: at 8.7% blended weighted-average cost of capital and ~$1B/year steady-state revenue, a 15-year round-trip yields ~+$0.76B net present value, a 20-year round-trip is marginal, and 25+ years is net-present-value-negative. Two architecture-matrix cells close at 15 years: Kilopower Variant B chunk-fed chemical, and megawatt all-electric. Matrix cells that violate 15 years were never economically viable; the ceiling clarifies the design space.) | A, T |
| L0-06 | The first delivery to customer SHALL occur no later than 20 years after program full-funding date. | First customer hand-off ≤ year 20, measured from contractual program-start date. (Bounds the patient-capital horizon. Tighter than L0-05 because it includes pre-mission build, launch wait, and first inbound coast.) | T |

### 7.5 Mission classes

Aerospace programs of this risk class retire technical and commercial risk through precursor missions before committing to the full architecture. Level 0 requirements in sections 1–6 above apply to **commercial-class missions at steady state.** Earlier mission classes operate under relaxed requirement sets that inherit selectively from Level 0 and are recorded here. The matrix in the architecture decision document maps to commercial-class only; precursor classes are off-matrix and are sized to retire specific risks at the lowest cost that closes the relevant gate.

| Class | Purpose | Inherits | Relaxed / waived |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Demonstrator-class** (first mission, or first 1–2 missions) | Retire technical risk on the harvest, return, and delivery loop. Prove the architecture works at *any* scale. | L0-01, L0-02, L0-03, L0-15, L0-16, L0-17 | L0-04 mass floor relaxed to "any non-zero mass delivered." L0-05, L0-06, L0-07, L0-08, L0-09 do not apply. L0-10, L0-11 reliability targets relaxed. L0-12 cost-competitiveness does not apply (demonstrator may have negative unit economics). |
| **Pre-commercial-class** (next 2–4 missions) | Retire commercial risk: customer hand-off, repeat customers, contract structure, insurance. Prove the *business* works at sub-commercial scale. | L0-01, L0-02, L0-03, L0-09 partial, L0-10 partial, L0-15, L0-16, L0-17 | L0-04 floor relaxed (sub-commercial mass acceptable). L0-07, L0-08 cadence requirements do not yet apply. L0-12 cost competitive within a factor of 2× target. |
| **Commercial-class** (steady-state, post-year-N) | Execute the steady-state business. | All Level 0 requirements. | None. |
| **Expansion-class** (optional, post-commercial-stable) | Higher mass classes per L0-20. | All Level 0 requirements. | L0-04 floor raised; no others affected. |

Each mission class has its own set of derived Level 1 requirements; those documents are out of scope here.

**Critical convention:** delivered mass for a demonstrator-class mission is *not* the same metric as L0-04 commercial-class mass. Conflating them is the canonical mistake in this type of program (overpromising what the first flight does, then under-delivering and torching the financing model). The variance log below tracks any waiver explicitly.

## 9. Variance protocol

When a Level 1 design study cannot meet a Level 0 requirement, the protocol is:

1. **Document the conflict** in the relevant round's STUDY.md, naming the Level 0 requirement.
2. **Re-derive the binding analysis** that produced the conflict.
3. **Propose a varied value** for the Level 0 metric, with the rationale (what the new value buys, what it costs).
4. **Reach explicit decision** with the project owner before treating the variance as accepted.
5. **Update this document** with the varied value, the rationale, the date, and a back-pointer to the round that drove the variance.

Variances accumulate as an audit trail in this section, not as silent edits to the table above.
