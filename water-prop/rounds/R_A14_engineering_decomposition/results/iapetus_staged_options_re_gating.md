# Iapetus staged-options re-gating (Step 7)

## Context

The iapetus staged-options framing (R-staged-options-with-technology-gates, R-T1-sensitivity-and-breakeven) structured ICEBERG as a four-tranche options program. Its tranche-1 gate (T1) was the **Fission Surface Power Phase-2 award** — an *external event* with a breakeven probability of 0.25 at $24B headline value vs a public-evidence high estimate of 0.023.

Two 2026-05-22 results change the gate structure:

1. **R-kilopower-scale-up-credibility (`3529984`, hyperion, this session)** resolved matrix decision #14 toward *hold the directive*: the 30-kilowatt-electric closure cell is mass-robust but programmatically near-zero, and the only path with a deliverable-looking posterior (single core, 1.5%) is Fission-Surface-Power-class, not Kilopower. **The FSP-2-award gate is therefore moot as a tranche-1 gate** — it gates an architecture (FSP-class reactor) that the directive does not admit, and even a Phase-2 award would not make 30 kWe "Kilopower."

2. **This round (R-A14-engineering-decomposition)** shows the chunk-capture bet (bet #1) is retired by an **internal, controllable demonstrator** — an Earth-orbit catch-and-contain test plus a Saturn small-chunk mission — not by an external award.

## Proposed tranche-1 gate replacement

Replace the external FSP-2-award gate with an **internal demonstrator gate** on the three engineering bets:

| Tranche-1 gate | Old (iapetus R7) | New (post-2026-05-22) |
|---|---|---|
| Gate event | FSP Phase-2 award (external) | Earth-orbit catch-and-contain demonstrator + water-electrothermal continuous-run demonstrator (internal) |
| Controllability | none (DoE/NASA decision) | full (ICEBERG-funded) |
| Baseline pass prob | 0.023–0.25 | catch+contain demonstrator success ~0.7–0.85 (deployable-target-mass test is low-novelty); A1 continuous-run demonstrator is the harder gate |
| What it retires | reactor program credibility (bet #3) | bet #1 (chunk capture) + bet #2 (water-electrothermal) |

## Where this leaves the three engineering bets after re-gating

- **Bet #1 (chunk capture):** retired by demonstrator. Demonstrator-conditional A14 joint = 0.69, clears the 25 t and 30 t floors at 200 t chunk. **Tranche-1 retirable.**
- **Bet #2 (continuous water-electrothermal at flight scale):** *not yet audited by a dedicated round* (flagged in locked finding 5 and in the R-kilopower handoff). The Saturn-worker A1 finding rates it PLAUSIBLE-PROVISIONAL (800 s ground-confirmed; continuous-months-on-Saturn-water flight gap remains). **This is now the highest-leverage un-audited bet** and the natural hardest part of the tranche-1 demonstrator.
- **Bet #3 (reactor program):** per R-kilopower-scale-up-credibility, this is the bet that does NOT retire via an ICEBERG-controllable demonstrator — it depends on an external space-fission program with a ≤1.5% delivery posterior. **This is the bet that should move OFF the critical path** (per matrix decision #14 → hold directive; the program is a tech-demonstrator, not a flight-reactor commitment).

## Reading for matrix decision #13 (pitch staged-options reframe)

The staged-options pitch should re-gate tranche 1 from "wait for FSP-2 award" to "fund an Earth-orbit catch-and-contain demonstrator + a continuous water-electrothermal demonstrator." This is a strictly stronger pitch position: the gate is internal and controllable (ICEBERG decides when to run it), the dominant-kill bet (#3, reactor) is removed from the critical path, and the two retained bets (#1, #2) have demonstrator paths with materially higher pass probability than the 0.023–0.25 FSP-2 gate. **The expected-loss number iapetus R7 computed against the FSP-2 gate should be recomputed against the internal demonstrator gate; it will improve.**
