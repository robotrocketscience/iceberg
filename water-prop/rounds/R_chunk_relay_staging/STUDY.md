## Results vs registered hypotheses

### H1 — "N=1 strictly loses everywhere" — **FALSIFIED (by real structure, in the waiver regime)**

The registered claim held at paper-κ and at every strict-schedule corner, but fell at flown-κ/20 kWe: a single-chunk relay with a *smaller* reactor than the monolithic's 30 kWe floor beats it by ~10 % (launch-per-delivered 2.17 vs 2.41) by taking a 6.8-year sortie under the 25-yr waiver. The monolithic cannot follow — its spread-spiral needs ≥ 24.7 kWe to finish within the 8-year transit. **The slow-and-light effect is stronger than registered: under the waiver, trading time for reactor mass pays even at N=1 when specific power is flown-anchored.** The strict-regime version of H1 survives intact.

### H2 — the multi-chunk optimum — **FALSIFIED (registration-grid mismatch, mechanism confirmed)**

Best waiver-legal cells beat the registered bands *from below*: **paper-κ 0.36 t/t at 60 kWe, N=5** (+65 % vs the monolithic's 1.02; residence 11.4 yr) and flown-κ 1.33 at 50 kWe, N=3 (+45 %). The pre-script's N ≤ 4 coarse grid missed the true optimum shape: **residence-saturating** — the largest N that fits the waiver at the smallest power that allows it. Both optima sit at minimum feasible power for their N, confirming the slow-and-light mechanism the band was built on. Third registration-grid mismatch of its class this session; convention amended: **pre-script grids must span the run grid, or bands must be registered one-sided.**

### H3 — the strict schedule kills it — **HELD**

The strict-N=2 power frontier bisects to **193 kWe**, where reactor mass drives launch-per-delivered to 1.88 vs the monolithic's 1.02; zero strict-legal cells beat the monolithic anywhere in the sweep. **The relay's entire advantage lives under the 25-yr L0-05 waiver** — the same waiver architecture E needed.

### H4 — harvest and exposure ledger — **HELD**

At the winning cells the relay harvests **2.9× chunk mass per delivered chunk vs the monolithic's 3.5×** — a genuine relief on bet #1's capture-count exposure — and the mothership crosses the ring plane zero times vs the monolithic's two per mission. At flown-κ high-power corners the harvest ratio degrades to ~7× (those corners lose anyway).
