# READING — R-demonstrator-mission-concept

## Load-bearing reading

**The demonstrator re-gates ICEBERG from an external-event bet to an internal-experiment bet — the biggest single improvement to the program's risk story this session produces.** iapetus R7 priced tranche-1 on the Fission-Surface-Power-Phase-2 award (external, ~2.3% pass, kills early >95% of the time). Defer the reactor (bet #3, ≤1.5% delivery, off critical path), fly a non-nuclear demonstrator that retires the two ICEBERG-controllable bets (#1 chunk capture, #2 water-electrothermal), and tranche-1 becomes an internal experiment at ~14× the pass probability.

**Honest caveat:** even with the cheap Earth-orbit proxy retiring bet #1 first, the demonstrator is a **~1-in-3 shot (0.33)** at retiring both on-path bets in one mission — dominated by bet #2's 0.48 continuous-months flight-readiness. Sequencing is what makes it defensible.

## Recommended demonstrator structure

1. **Earth-orbit catch-and-contain proxy FIRST** (cheap, pre-cruise) — lift bet #1 from 0.53 to 0.69 before committing the 12–15-year cruise. Highest-leverage gate.
2. **Continuous-months RF-ion run on bag-filtered chunk water during the Saturn cruise** — the dominant-kill long pole; the demonstrator IS bet #2's test article.
3. **Reactor deferred off critical path** — fly non-nuclear (RTG / solar-hybrid); retire bet #3 separately or via NASA Fission Surface Power before commercial-class commitment.

## Most consequential design decision (corrects the SCOPE)

The demonstrator must fly the **commercial water RF-ion (2000 s) + bag sublimation-distillation filtration stack** on dirty chunk water — NOT the power-appropriate MET (~543 s) a small low-power vehicle would naturally use. MET would prove water-electrothermal in deep space but not retire the contamination-sensitive RF-ion-continuous bet the commercial matrix rests on (bet-2 audit: MET 0–0.5% closure; cells run 2000 s RF-ion).
