Pointwise spare × shortfall = **0.0 exactly** at every trajectory point of all 80 swept corners. Spare power and keep-alive draw never coexist; recovery can only time-shift feedstock, never re-split with concurrent energy.

The R174–R178 venting convention was not merely conservative — it was **optimal**. R177's follow-on suggestion is falsified by its own successor round.

## Revisit (mandatory)

The theorem is a property of the smooth chain model: real missions have eclipses, attitude events, and load transients that interleave brief spare/draw windows the model cannot see — second-order (minutes-to-hours against a 3.7-year draw leg), flagged not priced. Recovery hardware on the *demonstrator* (1 AU, energy-rich, R180's envelope) faces no such theorem — but there feedstock was never scarce either, so the conclusion carries. Multi-mission fleet reuse of retained water was not in scope.
