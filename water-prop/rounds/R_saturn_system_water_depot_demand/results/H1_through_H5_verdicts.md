# H1–H5 verdicts — Saturn-system water-depot demand

**Worker:** hyperion (re-spawn 2). **Date:** 2026-05-26. Verdicts from the scored inventory (`demand_side_inventory.md`).

| # | Hypothesis | Verdict | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **H1** | Saturn-system demand structurally absent under 2026 baseline | **HELD (strong)** | The three live Saturn-destination missions (Dragonfly confirmed 2028/2034; Enceladus Orbilander ~2050s; ESA L4 Enceladus ~2050s) are all self-provisioned science missions with no in-space refueling step. No commercial actor has any Saturn-system architecture. There is no water customer at Saturn at any commitment level. |
| **H2** | Demand exists only conditional on future agency/commercial programs at ≤10% base rate | **HELD but MOOT** | Future Saturn programs do exist (the two Enceladus flagships) at ~0.3–0.5 materialisation — *above* the ≤10% the hypothesis posited. But the conditioning that matters for ICEBERG ("a multi-mission program that uses *local refueling*") is absent: even the committed/studied programs carry their own propellant. So demand is not gated on program probability — it is absent *regardless of* program success. H2's mechanism (probability-gated demand) does not apply; the demand is structurally zero, not merely low-probability. |
| **H3** | Demand exists at "fuel for missions crossing the Saturn system" (logistics market) | **FALSIFIED** | Δv-inverted. Using a Saturn depot costs ~15.4 km/s (capture ~7.7 + re-depart ~7.7, corrected vis-viva anchors) — far more than any flyby mission would save by not launching its own propellant. Saturn-destination missions arrive provisioned and have no large post-arrival Δv to refuel for. ≈0 net-positive customers/decade. Not a thin market; a non-market. |
| **H4** | Demand exists at "ICEBERG is its own customer" (two-vehicle relay) | **HELD-as-reframe, but weaker than SCOPE expected** | This is the only non-empty reading, and it is an architecture question, not a demand question — as the SCOPE anticipated. BUT the back-of-envelope shows the **relay re-imports the loaded-inbound reactor-lifetime / propulsion kill** that closed the one-vehicle architecture. The depot helps only the filler. So H4 does **not** obviously escape the binding constraint; it relocates it. A dedicated round is justified *only if* a relay-propulsion escape (depot-water-fed high-thrust sprint, or Earth aerocapture) is identified first. |
| **H5** | Saturn-system human-presence program at industrial scale before 2050 | **HELD (as stated: very-low-probability)** | No agency or commercial program contemplates Saturn-system human presence on any documented horizon. Conditioning event is far below any defensible 2026 prior; not before 2050. |

## Aggregate

- **No commercial market for water at Saturn exists or is forecastable at a 2026 baseline** (H1 strong, H2 moot, H3 falsified, H5 negligible).
- **The only architecture-internal reading (H4) does not cleanly escape the binding kill** — contrary to the SCOPE's expectation that H4 would be straightforwardly "worth a dedicated round."
- Therefore the load-bearing **H6** reading holds, and in a *stronger* form than the SCOPE pre-registered: option (b) is not commercially actionable under any reading, and the H4 escape hatch is itself conditional on a not-yet-identified relay-propulsion concept.
