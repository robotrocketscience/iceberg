## Two collapse mechanisms (honest decomposition)

Walking the 150 closed-loop cells, every cell fails for one of two reasons:

- **M1 — unlaunchable self-consistent vehicle (convention-robust in direction).** The derived dry
  mass for a 200 t chunk at 30 kWe is **120.8 t** → 603.9 t wet at the 0.80 convention. Max launch
  capacity in the framework is 300 t (6× Falcon Heavy) / 100 t (Starship). Most of the grid
  (any cell with derived wet > 300 t) is rejected at Phase 0. This direction does not depend on the
  propellant convention — a heavier dry mass only makes wet larger.
- **M2 — launchable vehicles fail the departure burn under the 0.80 propellant convention
  (convention-sensitive).** The launchable cells (derived wet ≤ 300 t, e.g. 1 kWe / 10 t chunk →
  138 t wet) cannot complete the 7.3 km/s chemical trans-Saturn injection: a 175.8 t stack needs
  137.4 t of chemical propellant but the 0.80 fraction provides 123.8 t (7.3 km/s at 340 s needs an
  ~88 % propellant fraction, not 80 %). Low-thrust escapes this only above 20 kWe **and** at high
  thrust (burn-time gate), and those cells are themselves unlaunchable (M1). **This mechanism is
  sensitive to decision D5** (frozen 0.80 propellant fraction): deriving propellant from the Δv
  schedule could let some launchable cells depart — but they would then be even heavier / need more
  launches. The *direction* (collapse) is robust; the *completeness* (literally 0 arrive) is partly a
  D5 artifact and is flagged, not over-claimed.

Derived dry / wet mass across the grid (independent of thrust at first-pass anchors):

| power (kWe) | 10 t chunk | 50 t chunk | 200 t chunk |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 27.7 t / 138 t | 42.2 t / 211 t | 96.7 t / 483 t |

All cells **converge** (H1; 6 iterations typical) — the collapse is from self-consistent mass being
large, not from numerical pathology.

## Coordination flags for the orchestrator

1. **Anchor deviation (D1).** Powerplant reuses the locked KRUSTY/MARVL anchors, not the SCOPE
   first-pass reactor form (which is ~2.5× lighter at 30 kWe). This is a deliberate consistency choice,
   not a >2× *error* in the SCOPE anchors as a class — but the SCOPE's reactor line specifically should
   be treated as superseded by the locked belief. Surfaced per SCOPE "Open coordination questions".
2. **D5 propellant-convention sensitivity.** The "literally 0 cells arrive" strength of the result is
   partly a consequence of holding the 0.80 propellant fraction while dry mass grows. The collapse
   *direction* is robust; a follow-on that derives propellant from the Δv schedule
   (R-vehicle-mass-fidelity-refinement) is needed before quoting "0 % arrive" as convention-independent.
3. **Path-conditional TPS (D3).** v1 charges aerocapture TPS on every vehicle (conservative). H5 sits
   near its bracket edge largely because of this. A path-conditional TPS charge is a fidelity follow-on.
